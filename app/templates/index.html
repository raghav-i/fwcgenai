What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin Type and Skin Diseases

Acne

A common skin condition characterized by the presence of pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads typically occurring on the face, chest, and back due to clogged pores and inflammation.

Acne Scars

Marks or depressions left on the skin after severe acne breakouts heal, resulting from damage to the skin's collagen fibers. They can vary in appearance and may fade over time but often require medical intervention for significant improvement.

Acanthosis Nigricans

A skin condition characterized by thickened, dark, velvety patches of skin typically occurring in the folds and creases of the body, often associated with insulin resistance, obesity, hormonal disorders, or certain medications.

Alopecia Areata

An autoimmune condition causing sudden hair loss in round patches on the scalp or other parts of the body, thought to be triggered by the immune system attacking hair follicles.

Melasma

A common skin condition causing brown or gray patches, typically on the face, triggered by hormonal changes, sun exposure, or genetics.

Vitiligo

A skin disorder characterized by patches of depigmented skin due to the loss of melanin-producing cells, resulting in irregular white patches on the skin's surface.

Warts

Small, benign growths caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), appearing on the skin's surface, commonly on the hands and feet, and may vary in appearance depending on the type of HPV involved.
